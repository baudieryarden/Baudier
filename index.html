Overview

My Yard — Layout & Microclimate

New Orleans Zone 9b backyard with a mature pecan canopy, large banana grove on the west side, two galvanized raised beds connected by a cow panel arch trellis, a small greenhouse, and a perennial border bed by the shed. The beds sit in the best sun corridor in the yard — open southern sky with roughly 6–7 hours of direct sun daily. Shade arrives from the northeast around 5pm.

☀️
Sun Corridor
Open southern sky above both beds. Morning sun from east from ~7am. Pecan + banana filter afternoon light. Shade hits around 5pm from northeast. Best sun: 10am–4pm.
🌳
Pecan Tree
Mature native pecan directly behind beds. Dense but gapped canopy. Provides mineral-rich leaf litter, hosts 200+ native insect species. Harvest October–December.
🍌
Banana Grove
Large grove on west side. Creates warm humid microclimate, acts as windbreak. Leaves are high-potassium compost tea gold. Plant turmeric and ginger at the base.
🏠
Greenhouse
Small greenhouse to the right of the beds. Use for seed starting December–February. Start tomatoes in December for February transplants. Perfect for lisianthus and snapdragons.
🌿
Food Forest Layers Already in Place Canopy (pecan) → Sub-canopy (banana, fig) → Shrub (border perennials) → Ground (raised beds) → Vine (arch trellis crops). Missing layers to add: understory fruiting shrubs (elderberry, pawpaw) and fungal layer (wine cap mushrooms at banana base).

The Raised Beds — What's Growing

🌸 Left Bed — Cut Flowers + Herbs

Oval/elongated galvanized bed. Currently home to the flower garden and herb collection with the left side of the arch trellis for tall climbers.

Big Bertha Peppers
Heat-loving, prolific producer through fall. Keep well watered. Will produce heavily September–November. No additional nitrogen needed — beans in right bed are fixing plenty.

Chadwick Tomato
Heirloom cherry type, very vigorous indeterminate. Currently has fruit not yet ripening — normal for June heat. Cut back 1/3 in late August for fall flush. Cage may need supplementing as it grows.

Basil
Perfect companion for tomatoes and peppers. Pinch flower buds as they appear to keep leafy. Thrives in summer heat. Harvest regularly — cut to just above a leaf pair.

Butterfly host

Perennial in 9b. Will spread slowly to fill gaps — let them. Flowers are edible and attract pollinators. Excellent companion deterring aphids. Divide every few years to reinvigorate.

Fennel Warning — Remove from Beds Fennel releases allelopathic chemicals that inhibit tomatoes, beans, lisianthus, and most vegetables. Move fennel to its own container away from both beds. It will be happier isolated and so will everything else.

Tomatoes — Troubleshooting & Variety Guide

🌡️
Why New Orleans Tomatoes Stall in Summer Above 90°F daytime and 75°F nighttime, tomato pollen becomes sterile and fruit cannot ripen (can't produce lycopene above 95°F). This is normal. Your real tomato harvest windows are: March–June and September–November. Plants going in April are racing the clock — next year start in December in the greenhouse and transplant February.

Bloom Booster Protocol for Reluctant Tomatoes

5-Step Flowering Fix
  1. Stop all nitrogen fertilizer — the bean/peanut bed is already adding too much
  2. Switch to high-phosphorus feed — look for middle number highest on label (e.g. 5-10-5)
  3. Epsom salt spray — 1 tbsp per gallon, spray leaves early morning weekly
  4. Shake plants gently each morning or use electric toothbrush against stems — mimics bee vibration, helps set any open flowers
  5. Deep water less frequently — slight water stress signals plant to reproduce rather than grow vegetatively
